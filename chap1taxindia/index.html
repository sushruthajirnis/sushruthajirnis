Section 1 · The Foundation
3 Rules.
That's All.
①
Name: "Income-tax Act, 2025" — replaces the 1961 Act.
②
Where: "It extends to the whole of India" — including territorial waters, seabed, continental shelf, exclusive economic zone, and airspace.
③
When: Came into force on 1st April, 2026. Presidential assent was earlier — 21 Aug 2025.

Section 2(11) · Assessee
Kya
Tumhe Tax
Bharna
Padta Hai?
If you are an assessee, yes. The Act defines it as anyone by whom any tax or any sum of money is payable under this Act. It also includes people whose income is being assessed — even if no tax is due.
🙋
Are YOU an assessee?
The short answer
If you file returns, owe any amount, or have had any income-tax proceeding started against you — yes, you are an assessee.

Section 3 · Tax Year
Big
Change
from 1961
The old law had a confusing split: "Previous Year" (when you earn) vs "Assessment Year" (when you're taxed). The 2025 Act merges them into one concept:
Apr 1 → Mar 31
One "Tax Year" = 12 months
🚀
Started a business on 15 Oct 2026?
§ 3(2)(a)
Your first tax year runs from 15 Oct 2026 → 31 Mar 2027. Not a full year — just until March 31.

Section 2(5) · Agricultural Income
Farmer
ka Hisaab
Agricultural income is defined — not automatically exempt. The definition matters because only what fits §2(5) can claim that status.

✓

✓
Processing produce to make it market-ready (husking, etc.)

✗
Renting farmhouse as holiday home or for business = NOT agricultural income

Section 2(111) · Virtual Digital Assets
Crypto
is Now
Law.
For the first time in Indian tax law, a full legal definition. It covers 4 sub-clauses:
①
Crypto / Tokens — Bitcoin, Ethereum, meme coins: any cryptographically-generated token with value that can be transferred or traded electronically
②
NFTs — "non-fungible token or any other token of similar nature, by whatever name called"
③
Future assets — Govt can notify new digital asset types via notification
④
Blockchain assets — "relies on a cryptographically secured distributed ledger"

When did the Income-tax Act, 2025 come into force?
✅ 1 April 2026. Section 1(3) states: "it shall come into force on the 1st April, 2026." The 21 Aug 2025 date was Presidential assent — different from when it became operative. § 1(3) — Page 1
Quiz · 2 of 5
KBC Jeet
— Taxable?
Priya wins ₹10 lakh on a popular TV quiz show. Is this taxable income?
✅ Yes, fully taxable. § 2(49)(n) covers "card games and other games of any sort." § 2(49)(A) clarifies this "includes any game show, an entertainment programme on television or electronic mode, in which people compete to win prizes." § 2(49)(n)(A) — Pages 15–16
Quiz · 3 of 5
Shares
Held
10 Months
Amit buys Reliance shares listed on NSE in Jan 2026, sells in Nov 2026. Short-term or long-term?
✅ Short-term. The general rule is 24 months, but § 2(101)(b)(i) reduces this to 12 months for securities listed on a recognised stock exchange. Amit held 10 months → Short-Term Capital Asset. § 2(101)(b)(i) — Page 21
Quiz · 4 of 5
Farm
+ Holiday
Home
Ramesh farms wheat AND rents his farmhouse to city couples as a weekend getaway. Which income is agricultural?
✅ Only the farming income. § 2(5)(c)(i) explicitly excludes income from a farm building used "for any purpose (including letting for residential purpose…) other than agriculture." Renting as holiday home = residential purpose = taxable income. § 2(5)(c)(i) — Page 2
Quiz · 5 of 5
New
Business,
New Year
Neha starts a cloud kitchen on 10 August 2026. When does her first tax year begin and end?
✅ 10 Aug 2026 to 31 Mar 2027. § 3(2)(a): "the tax year shall be the period beginning with the date of setting up of such business or profession… and ending with the said financial year." Financial year ends on 31 March. § 3(2)(a) — Page 26
